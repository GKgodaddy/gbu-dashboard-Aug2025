üö® THE UGLY

Microsoft Brazil Payment Crisis Continues
We've faced persistent issues with Microsoft rejecting our Brazil invoice payments for over a year, with rejected payments now exceeding $1M. In July, Microsoft revealed the rejections stemmed from their changing of GoDaddy's country of origin during NCE migration. Despite our payment and MSFT acceptance of a $1M payment, new registrations in Brazil are still blocked.
Owner:
Michele Dugan, David Woolf, Ryan Gist, Dom Nelson, Ryan Beal, Katy Kendall
Action:
We are currently working with GoDaddy legal and Microsoft partner management to resolve these critical issues and determine next steps, including reissuing invoices to the correct entity. We also expanded our analysis of entity alignment with MSFT and identified potential issues to address as exceptions, or alternative entity sales paths.
Immediate Action Required
Australian GST Group Lodgement Compliance Gap
Statutory audit firm identified that we do not have a indirect tax sharing agreement for GoDaddy Online Services Australia (CMP005), which prepares a GST group lodgement and payment on behalf of multiple GD entities that are not part of it's individual statutory financial statements. Disallowed input tax credits (ITCs) are recognized in CMP005 and not pushed back to the related entity.
Owner:
David Woolf, Katy Kendall, Catherine Hodges, Froy Leon
Action:
Consulting with KPMG to breakout the B/S and P&L impacts specific to CMP005, evaulated for materiality and prior year impacts and post adjustments, if necessary, in order to finalize the audit.
Immediate Action Required

Overall Assessment

Close to Report demonstrates strong team development capabilities while managing complex international crises. Team rotation successes show operational maturity, while persistent payment and compliance issues highlight the complexity of global operations requiring sustained cross-functional coordination.
