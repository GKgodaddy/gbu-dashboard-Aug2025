Global Tax Refunds & Reductions

Colombia Tax Refund Success - USD $1.28 Million
On August 26, 2025, we successfully obtained approval from the Colombian Tax Authority for a tax refund of approximately COP 5 billion (around USD 1.28 million). This was achieved by strategically negotiating application fees with our local service provider in advance and submitting the application promptly after filing the TY2024 CIT return in April 2025. We expect to sell the TIDIS certificate for cash by mid-September and properly updated the 2025 8+4 Cash Tax Forecast.
Medium Impact
Contributors:
Jack Deng, Jennifer Decker
Netherlands NOL Utilization - USD $816K Refund
In the Netherlands, we have researched, documented, and supported the utilization of approximately EUR 1.3 million in NOL, resulting in a total cash tax refund of around EUR 700,000 (approximately USD 816,000) for TY2022 and TY2023, which we expect to receive in December 2025. 2025 8+4 Cash Tax Forecast is updated and noted for this change accordingly.
Medium Impact
Contributors:
Alex Lukin, Jack Deng, Jennifer Decker
Germany & Israel Tax Optimization - USD $1 Million
For Germany, we have thoroughly reviewed the current year's estimated tax liabilities and tax refund positions for TY2021-TY2025, resulting in a reduction of cash tax outflow in 2025 by approximately EUR 900,000 (around USD 1 million). In Israel, we have significantly reduced CIT liabilities by over 77% going forward by securing SBC deductions that we had previously been unable to deduct. 2025 8+4 Cash Tax Forecast is updated and noted for these changes accordingly.
Medium Impact

Microsoft Brazil Payment Crisis Continues
We've faced persistent issues with Microsoft rejecting our Brazil invoice payments for over a year, with rejected payments now exceeding $1M. In July, Microsoft revealed the rejections stemmed from their changing of GoDaddy's country of origin during NCE migration. Despite our payment and MSFT acceptance of a $1M payment, new registrations in Brazil are still blocked.
Owner:
Michele Dugan, David Woolf, Ryan Gist, Dom Nelson, Ryan Beal, Katy Kendall
Action:
We are currently working with GoDaddy legal and Microsoft partner management to resolve these critical issues and determine next steps, including reissuing invoices to the correct entity. We also expanded our analysis of entity alignment with MSFT and identified potential issues to address as exceptions, or alternative entity sales paths.
Immediate Action Required
Australian GST Group Lodgement Compliance Gap
Statutory audit firm identified that we do not have a indirect tax sharing agreement for GoDaddy Online Services Australia (CMP005), which prepares a GST group lodgement and payment on behalf of multiple GD entities that are not part of it's individual statutory financial statements. Disallowed input tax credits (ITCs) are recognized in CMP005 and not pushed back to the related entity.
Owner:
David Woolf, Katy Kendall, Catherine Hodges, Froy Leon
Action:
Consulting with KPMG to breakout the B/S and P&L impacts specific to CMP005, evaulated for materiality and prior year impacts and post adjustments, if necessary, in order to finalize the audit.
Immediate Action Required
